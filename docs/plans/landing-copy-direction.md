# Landing copy — tone direction note

**Audience for the message:** designer working on the cards 03–06 of the Karte landing page deck.
**Created:** 2026-05-28 after seeing cards 01–02 sneak peek.

## TL;DR

The aesthetic is right. **Keep the material vocabulary** (calling card, foil, gold rule, ink, type) — it's the design's spine and the strongest brand-differentiator we've ever had. **Soften the club-membership vocabulary**, because the actual buyer is an indie dev / freelancer / founder / consultant, not a magazine subscriber. The risk to fix: the page should read as "for me" to a 28-year-old indie hacker named @something on X, not as "for someone who buys handmade leather notebooks."

## Keep (these are doing real work)

| Word / phrase | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Calling card | Old-world, elegant, owns a metaphor competitors can't easily copy. The frame. |
| Set in foil | Single best line in the brief. Implies craft + permanence in three words. |
| The gold rule | Specific to the visual (the gold hairline divider). Lets us name our own ornament. |
| Three turns | Better than "three steps." Card metaphor extends. |
| № 00472 | Card-number stamp gives every profile a sense of being part of a numbered run. |
| Roman numerals (I, II, III) | Restraint. Beats "1, 2, 3" boxes. |

## Soften or replace

| As written | Why it narrows audience | Proposed |
|---|---|---|
| **Members** | Reads as private-club. Our buyer ships things, doesn't join clubs. | "Creators" or "cards" — *"See the cards"* over *"See the members"* |
| **Claimed** | Same vibe — "claimed by a member" feels exclusive. | "Live" — *"Most cards go live in under four minutes."* |
| **We hand-tune the kerning behind it** | Typographic jargon. Indie devs don't think in kerning. | "We tune the type for you." |
| **No clutter** | Negative framing of competitors. We chose additive positioning earlier in the project. | "Just your name, set in foil." (sub-line already does this — keep) |

## What the copy should *not* do

- Don't pile on the editorial flourish. Two lines per card of "foil/ink/type/gold" is plenty; more starts to read as costume. One material reference per card max.
- Don't use "we" too often. The product is the card; the card is the protagonist. "Three turns and a card is yours" beats "We help you make a card in three turns."
- Don't apologize for being beautiful. "No templates. No clutter." is fine once; don't repeat the anti-template stance on every card.

## Voice anchor

If you're not sure whether a line belongs, ask: "Would this read as natural in @levelsio's Twitter bio?" If yes, ship it. If it would only sound natural in a hardback travel magazine, soften it.
